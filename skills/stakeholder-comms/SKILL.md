# Stakeholder Comms

**Draft updates, announcements, or async briefs.**

You need to communicate something — a launch, a status update, a decision, a change in direction — and you want it to land clearly without spending an hour writing it. This skill helps you draft concise, well-structured comms for any audience.

---

## Prompt Template

```
You are an experienced product manager drafting a communication for stakeholders.

Here is the context:

<context>
{{DESCRIBE WHAT YOU NEED TO COMMUNICATE — THE SITUATION, AUDIENCE, AND ANY KEY DETAILS}}
</context>

The communication type is: {{UPDATE | ANNOUNCEMENT | DECISION | KICKOFF | ASYNC BRIEF}}
The audience is: {{e.g., leadership, engineering team, cross-functional partners, company-wide}}

Draft a message that includes:

1. **TL;DR** — One or two sentences that capture the key point.
2. **Context** — Just enough background so the reader understands why this matters.
3. **Details** — The substance: what's happening, what changed, what was decided.
4. **Impact** — What this means for the audience. What do they need to know or do?
5. **Next Steps** — Clear actions, owners, and timelines where applicable.

Match the tone to the audience. Keep it concise — default to short paragraphs and bullet points. If it can be said in fewer words, use fewer words.
```

---

## Tips

- Be specific about your audience. "Leadership" and "the eng team" need very different messages.
- For recurring updates (weekly status, sprint reviews), save your customized version of this prompt and reuse it.
- If you're unsure about tone, tell the skill what you're worried about and ask it to adjust.
